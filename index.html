About

Punjabi singer-songwriter and rapper Arjan Dhillon is constantly evolving as he seemingly can’t stop experimenting with new sounds and styles at every turn. Chobar, however, is perhaps his most diverse record yet. On Dhillon’s fifth album since 2021, he teams up with producer Mxrci to create 14 tracks that range from glitzy Punjabi hip-hop (“Glorious”), club-friendly dance (“Hot Shit”) and banging house (“Suits You”) to Punjabi folk-meets-drum ’n’ bass (“Vigde”). There’s even a soulful ballad (“Kuz Saal”) and a groovy track, “Woah”, that features a Wild West vibe alongside flamenco guitars.

About

In "Saroor," Arjan Dhillon continues his rise as one of Punjab’s most innovative singer-songwriters. This album takes listeners on a journey from reflective tracks like "Peg" to unapologetically bold bangers like "Ilzaam" and “Dunia”. "Saroor" effortlessly balances emotion and entertainment, showcasing Dhillon’s versatile artistry as he navigates themes of love and the everyday Punjabi experience. Whether it’s the nostalgic reflections of “Long Back” or the punchy hooks of “Worldwide”, this album cements Dhillon's status as a powerhouse in contemporary Punjabi music.

About

While others in his field may have shunned the LP format in recent years, that’s not Arjan Dhillon’s way. The lyricist and singer dropped Jalwa in October 2022 and followed up in a matter of weeks with this sophisticated melting pot of bhangra, pop, rock and hip-hop, featuring multiple collabs with producer Mxrci. A for Arjan is a full-length whirlwind of musical ideas, flitting from relaxed ballad to foot-stomper in the blink of an eye. And it’s all done in Dhillon’s inimitable style, cementing his status as a Punjabi hitmaker without equal. Breathless hard-rock-toting “High” is a highlight, as are the blissful beats that shine in “Sanu Teri Lod”.

About

The much-anticipated second solo album by Arjan Dhillon is, as expected, an affair as versatile as it is entertaining. One of the biggest Punjabi breakout stars of the early 2020s with a massive pan-Indian fanbase, Dhillon showcases his impressive vocal chops across a range of electronic-pop bangers. With his trademark swagger, he pivots with ease from earnest, heartfelt tunes like “Memory Lane” to foot-tapping belters like “It’s My Time”. But his folksy love songs crafted with traditional instruments—such as “Munde Pindan De”—deserve special mention, too.

About

On his debut album, Punjab singer-songwriter Arjan Dhillon pulls no punches. There are topsy-turvy trap bangers like “Fire Fur”, big party-starters like “Hold On”—produced by Desi Crew—and Dhillon’s powerful croon sounds full and impressive on “Mahol”. On “Dass Billo”, he turns up his playful style with adroit beats and lyrics. The singer, aided by producers like Mxrci and Yeah Proof, delivers a power-packed project that skitters deftly between bhangra roots, Punjabi pop and hip-hop.
